## 2. Industry Analysis

### Competitive Position
- **Market share:** ~80-90% datacenter AI GPUs (near-monopoly)
- **Moat type:** Ecosystem (CUDA), performance lead, talent
- **Key risk:** Custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia)

### Industry Dynamics
| Factor | Current State | Trajectory |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| **Cycle** | Peak growth | Possible normalization 2026 |
| **Consolidation** | NVDA dominant | AMD distant second, Intel struggling |
| **Regulation** | Export controls on China | Tightening |
| **Disruption** | Open-source models, efficiency gains | DeepSeek shows less compute works |

### Porter's Forces Applied
- **Rivalry:** Low (AMD far behind, Intel irrelevant)
- **Substitutes:** MEDIUM — custom ASICs, efficiency improvements (DeepSeek V3)
- **Buyer power:** MEDIUM — hyperscalers are concentrated, could negotiate/substitute
- **Supplier power:** HIGH — TSMC dependency is critical
- **Entry barriers:** VERY HIGH — CUDA ecosystem, 10+ years of software

## 6. Risk/Reward Framework

### Scenario Analysis

| Scenario | Probability | 2-Year Return | Key Assumption |
|----------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Bull** | 25% | +80% | AI capex accelerates, Blackwell smooth, no competition |
| **Base** | 45% | +15% | Growth normalizes, multiple compresses, still dominates |
| **Bear** | 25% | -30% | Capex pause, custom silicon gains share, China restrictions bite |
| **Catastrophic** | 5% | -60% | Efficiency breakthrough reduces GPU demand (DeepSeek 10x) |

### Fragility Assessment

**Fragile factors:**
- TSMC single-source dependency
- 4 customers = >50% revenue
- China exposure (~15-20%)
- Key-man (Jensen)
- Extreme expectations priced in

**Antifragile factors:**
- Benefits from AI adoption volatility (both directions need GPUs)
- Software ecosystem stickiness
- R&D scale advantages

**Net:** MEDIUM-HIGH fragility at current valuation
